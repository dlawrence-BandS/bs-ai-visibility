What this dashboard tracks

Every Monday morning, a script sends ~100 customer-style questions to ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, records their answers, and asks Claude to score each response for how it treats Barker & Stonehouse. The results feed this dashboard.

The prompts fall into five categories:

  • Discovery — the customer doesn't know which brand to buy yet ("best UK sofa brands", "where to buy quality dining tables"). This is where B&S needs to surface if AI is going to bring in new customers.
  • Brand-specific — the customer already knows B&S ("is Barker and Stonehouse good quality"). Almost always mentions us, so mention rate here is a distraction.
  • Competitor — the question centres on a rival ("alternatives to DFS", "John Lewis vs Barker & Stonehouse"). Are we being suggested as an alternative?
  • Product — buying-intent questions ("best leather sofa under £3000", "Ercol dining table UK").
  • Trust — friction points ("is Barker & Stonehouse trustworthy", "delivery complaints"). Sentiment matters here more than mention rate.

Which numbers should I watch?

Default scope: non-brand prompts only

By default the executive view excludes brand-specific and trust prompts. Why: those prompts contain the phrase "Barker & Stonehouse" in the question itself, so AI names us in 95%+ of them — that flatters mention rate and share-of-voice without telling you anything you don't already know. The default scope shows only discovery, competitor and product prompts, which is the fair test of whether AI thinks of B&S unprompted.

Tick Include brand prompts at the top of the exec view if you specifically want to check how AI is responding to brand or trust questions (e.g. sentiment on "Barker and Stonehouse complaints" — that's the trust-category use case).

The four headline KPIs

  • Mention rate (scope-aware) — % of prompts in the current scope where AI named B&S. Reflects the toggle.
  • Non-brand mention rate — always excludes brand/trust regardless of toggle. This is the strategic KPI to track over time.
  • Share of voice — B&S's slice of all furniture-brand mentions AI made in the current scope. Compare against known market leaders like DFS/John Lewis.
  • Positive sentiment — when B&S is mentioned, how favourably. Above 70% is healthy for premium retail.

What "good" looks like

Baseline benchmarks for a regional premium retailer:

  • Non-brand mention rate 30–50% — solid; below 20% suggests a discoverability problem
  • Share of voice 5–10% — strong for a regional player; DFS/John Lewis will likely be 15–25%
  • Positive sentiment 70–85% — healthy; below 60% needs investigation
  • Avg rank 2–4 (visible in the competitor deep-dive) — you're in the consideration set

How to use this dashboard

Weekly check (5 minutes)

Open the Executive summary, hit Refresh data, and eyeball the four headline KPIs. Look for arrows: any big movement week-on-week deserves a look. Skim the category chart — where's the weakest bar?

Deep review (20 minutes, monthly)

  1. Executive summary → check trends and headline direction
  2. Full detail → Gap analysis: which specific prompts are we invisible on? Feed to SEO/content.
  3. Full detail → Claims log: any red claims? These are website-content issues to fix.
  4. Full detail → Every prompt: pick 3-4 rows, open the drawer, read the actual responses. That's how you understand what AI is saying about the brand.

Filtering

The three dropdowns at the top narrow the whole dashboard:

  • Look back — latest run only for a snapshot, or last 4-26 runs for trends
  • Model — useful for spotting when one AI provider treats you very differently from the others
  • Category — drill into a single prompt type

Answers for James

The three sentences to lead with each month:

  1. "AI mentions B&S in X% of non-brand searches, [up/down] Y percentage points vs last month."
  2. "We rank [N] in furniture share-of-voice, ahead of [competitor A] but behind [competitor B]."
  3. "The three biggest opportunities are [category] — where we're mentioned in only Z% of relevant answers — and [specific claim] which needs correcting on our category pages."

Everything on the dashboard supports one of those three sentences.

Caveats

Sample size: ~100 prompts is not a scientific sample of every possible customer question. It's a curated corpus. Add or remove prompts in prompts.yaml in the GitHub repo — the runner picks up changes on the next Monday.

Model drift: AI providers change their models. When gpt-5 or claude-opus-5 lands, the numbers may shift as much from model behaviour change as from real-world change. That's why we track by model.

Scoring reliability: Claude scores every response. It's mostly right but occasionally miscategorises sentiment or misses a mention buried in prose. Sanity-check by clicking through actual responses in the Every prompt tab.

Not causal: A mention rate going up doesn't automatically mean more revenue. It means AI is aware of B&S. Correlate with the LLM traffic dashboard (bs-llm) to see if awareness turns into visits.
